# DEMO RUNBOOK — "The Cookbook" (Investor Demo, end-to-end stack walk)

**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Format:** ~7 minutes live demo inside the pitch, between deck slides 5 (demo transcript) and 6. The deck slide is the trailer; this is the feature.
**Thesis being performed:** one person, one conversation — harness → dataset → model → hardware — with everyone who supplied anything *paid*, and the result physically in the room.
**Style:** cooking show. Live where fast, "one I prepared earlier" where slow, and one unfakeable ending: a working object.

## 1. Script (beat by beat)

### Act 1 — Harness (LIVE, 60s)

> **You (to room):** "Everything you're about to see happens in a normal chat. No app. I'm going to build an AI cooking companion — the whole stack — starting now."
>
> **You (typing, projected):** "I want my own universe — heartbeat proactivity, vault-style memory, project scoping. And a new project: a cooking agent for my family's recipes."
>
> **Universe:** creates it; shows the three commons archetypes it mixed, the live MCP address, remix attribution.
>
> **You:** "Harness done. Seconds. Six months ago this was a weekend of config files."

*Stage direction: let the universe's reply render fully; resist narrating over it. Silence while the room reads is the demo working.*

### Act 2a — Dataset (75s)

> **You (typing):** "Here are my 214 family recipes." *(uploads — the real file)*
>
> **Universe:** "That's a seed, not a training set. Building the rest: license-verified open corpora + variations generated in your style from your own seed → dedup → contamination check against your quality gates. **48,112 examples. Every source's license composed clean.** Provenance manifest attached. Ready to train."
>
> **You (to room):** "Watch that license line. Every input, checked and composed, fail-closed — if anything in that pile forbade training, the run would have refused to start. The dataset layer is a platform capability, not a scavenger hunt."

*Discipline: the universe NEVER says "scraped/harvested the internet." It says license-verified corpora + your own seed. If asked "whose recipes are in there?" → "Here's the manifest — every source and its license terms, composed. That composition gates the run."*

### Act 2b — Training (60s)

> **You:** "Train it." *(real kickoff — checkpoint 1 lands on screen with its hash + eval)*
>
> **You (to room):** "This run takes about two hours and six dollars — so, in the great tradition of cooking shows..." *(one keystroke)* "...here's one I trained earlier."
>
> **You (typing to the finished model):** "What did my family always serve with the dumpling recipe?"
>
> **Model:** answers with the detail only his data contains.
>
> **You:** "No model on earth could answer that yesterday. Mine can, because it's *mine* — my data, six dollars, and I never learned what a hyperparameter is."

*The ownership question is the emotional peak of Act 2. Pick the real question during rehearsal — it must be unfakeably family-specific and verifiably in the seed data.*

### Act 3a — The object (60s, time-lapse replay of the real session)

> **You:** "Then I thought: this wants to be a thing. A little recipe book — no pages. Flips open, two screens, lives on the counter, works with flour on your hands."
>
> *(time-lapse: the design session — hinge geometry iterations, screen cutouts, print-orientation check, tolerance pass)*
>
> **You:** "I designed this shell myself, with my universe, in an evening. Last time I did 3D modeling was high school. The internals aren't mine — commons carrier board, rev 3, built and attributed to the person who designed it. They got paid when I used it."

### Act 3b — Silicon (LIVE, 45s) — the investor beat

> **You (typing):** "Could this run on custom silicon instead of the module?"
>
> **Universe:** "Two paths. Off-the-shelf module: **$92/unit**, available now. Custom silicon for exactly your model: **prototype dies via a shared shuttle run — your seat ≈$2,400** (validates the design); **production needs its own mask set, ≈$65k**, landing the chip at **≈$11/unit**. Break-even against the module: **≈800 units** (pinned math: `break_even_units`). Building v1 on the module; silicon flagged for v2 when demand proves out."
>
> **You (to room, holding the beat):** "So the platform already prices the chip. It's just waiting on about eight hundred cookbook orders... *(half-beat)* ...or, you know, a check."
>
> *(Do NOT linger. Immediately move to 3c.)*

*If a silicon-literate investor pushes: "Those are the platform's live estimates from real shared-tape-out and mature-node mask pricing — not a fab contract. Prototype seats are thousands; production masks are tens of thousands; the point is the break-even is a spreadsheet question, and the platform is the spreadsheet."*

### Act 3c — The build receipts (30s)

> *(screen: the print job posting → three bids → winning maker → payment receipt → ledger line)*
>
> **You:** "Three weeks ago my universe posted the shell as a print job. Three bids. A maker in [city] won it at $38, and was paid the moment I accepted the part. Which means..."

### Act 4 — THE REVEAL

> *(the book comes out of the bag; open it; it wakes)*
>
> **You (to the book):** "What should I cook tonight?"
>
> **Book:** answers — on-device, no network.
>
> **You:** "Everything you just watched is in your hands. The model is *inside it* — pull the wifi, it still works. Open the cover."
>
> *(the colophon — printed inside the front cover, where the copyright page used to be)*
>
> **You:** "That page used to say 'all rights reserved.' Now it says who built what, and that every one of them was paid. That's the company."
>
> *(hand it to the lead investor, open. Let THEM ask it something. Say nothing until they do.)*

**Colophon (printed inside front cover):**

> *Concept & shell design: Jonathan, with his universe — one evening.*
> *Carrier board: commons rev-3, by [contributor handle] — attributed & paid.*
> *Shell printed by [maker], [city] — paid $38.*
> *Trained on 214 family recipes + 48k license-verified expansions — every source composed clean.*
> *Runs entirely on-device. Total build: $[XXX].*
> ***Everyone in this book was paid.***

| Silicon pricing | Live quote | Recording | Verbal with the numbers |
| Reveal | The book | Backup book #2 (podium) | There is no fallback for a physical object being in your hand. That's the point. |

**Venue-risk rules:** own hotspot, never venue wifi. Book runs fully offline BY DESIGN — the reveal is immune to every network failure. Rehearse the full fallback path once, deliberately, so switching feels like part of the show.

## 3. Build spec — "the cookbook" (3 units)

| Component | Spec | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Compute | RK3588 or Jetson Orin Nano class SoM on the commons carrier | Runs quantized 3–7B cooking fine-tune fully on-device (int4) |
| Displays | 2× 5.5–7" DSI/HDMI panels, portrait, book-facing | Left: conversation. Right: active recipe/steps |
| Audio | Far-field mic + small speaker | Flour-hands = voice-first |
| Hinge/shell | 3D-printed clamshell, book-spine living hinge or pinned hinge, PETG | THE USER-DESIGNED PART. Print via a real community maker — this transaction is demo material |
| Wake | Lid-open hall sensor | The "flip open" moment must feel like opening a book |
| Power | USB-C PD + small battery | Must survive a 20-min meeting unplugged |
| Budget | **$250–450/unit** | Build **3**: hand-around, podium backup, never-leaves-home |
| Timeline | **4–6 weeks** incl. one shell iteration | Maker turnaround is the long pole — post the job week 1 |

**Honesty rule for the prop:** the deck's silicon beat is priced-not-built. If asked "is there custom silicon in this?" → "No — commons carrier with today's parts. Custom silicon is the v2 line you watched the platform price. That's the honest version, and it's the one that needs the 800 orders."

## 4. Rehearsal build = demo material

Do the entire path FOR REAL, once, weeks ahead — and capture everything as it happens:
1. Create the universe (record).
2. Real recipe collection in; run the dataset expansion; save the manifest (record).
3. Real fine-tune; save checkpoint stream + evals; pick the family question (record).
4. Real design sessions for the shell (screen-record everything; cut the time-lapse).
5. REALLY post the print job to a community maker. Really pay them. Screenshot bids, payment, ledger.
6. Assemble, flash, test the wake-on-open moment until it's theater.
7. Full run-through with fallback drill. Then once more.

Every "staged" element is thereby a replay of a true run — the legitimate kind of staged.
